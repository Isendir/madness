Redesign 2012-06-24 23:50 News Technology

A few days ago, for a plaetora of reasons, I tried to upgrade my Octopress to a newer version. I failed, miserably. It served me well for a year, but past and current annoyances triggered a drastical move: away from it.

There always were annoying little things that bothered me about Octopress: from the language, through the complex and fragile update mechanism, to the silly source layout.

To this day, I do not understand why it has to be so complex. And for that reason, out of frustration, I am now in the middle of redesigning and rearchitecting this site from scratch. I'm building not only the design, but the static site generator too this time.

Why? one might rightfully ask - there are a million generators out there! And that is true, but none that I checked was easy enough to use, or if it was, it did not provide all the features I need. Neither did Octopress, by the way...

So, here I am, in the middle of it all, and I already have something built with Bootstrap, enlive and Clojure.

With the new system, I have a modern layout, which I find much easier to tweak (it's a single file, afterall), I have proper per-tag Atom feeds, gained quite a bit of control on how my site is built up, and it also generates noticably faster. Oh, and I do not need ruby for anything, anymore!
