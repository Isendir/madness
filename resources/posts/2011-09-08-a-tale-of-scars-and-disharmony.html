A tale of scars and disharmony... 2011-09-08 01:45 Fiction The Pilgrim

"You'll see a hundred or more people standing in front of a broken image, wondering who might have stolen their courage to look at the picture beneath."

A winter's night it was, when the first rays of the dawn started to pierce through the forest. On the icy lake among the trees, one could almost see a faint figure, as she was skating on the surface: sometimes gently, like if she didn't even touch the ice, sometimes so hard as if she was melting the ice on behalf of the rising sun.

Meanwhile, a tall, dark figure was rapidly approaching through the woods.

The ice was melting, the previous day weakened it already, it was a wonder it lasted so long as it is. Then it yielded, right under Her feet. Her terrified scream echoed through the surrounding mountains, and the dark figure began to run.

He threw away his cape, and dived under to find the girl. Find her, he did, but she was frozen through, the spark of life barely flickering in her heart. He took her in his arms, wrapped his warm cape around her, and headed towards the fisherman's cave.

This was the first time he looked at her, and for a moment, he stood dumbfolded: it was an Angel he held in his arms. His heart began to race, to pound and roar like a trapped bear. Her face, he'll never forget.

Shortly, he arrived, and the fisherman and his wife took the girl inside. The dark figure just stood there, thinking. Then he took his cape, and left without even looking back.
