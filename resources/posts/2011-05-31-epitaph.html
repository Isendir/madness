Epitaph 2011-05-31 02:26 Fiction The Pilgrim

Every legend has a beginning, and everything that ever existed, that ever began, always comes to an end.

Some pass away with fanfare,
buried in a timeless lair,
some quietly disappear,
ingenious and sincere.

And so has the Master gone, to places beyond mortal comprehension, his garden left behind to forever grow.

A spark of life beneath the earth,
a sprig of green, of joy and mirth,
a new tree born upon the grave:
an oak of love, of wisdom, of rave.

Many have tried to carve a few words onto his headstone, to pay respect, but all poems disappeared overnight, until one day, The Pilgrim wrote these words upon the wood:

When the Night, onto a rotten log, a new face he paints,
this last work of her love, the Dusk, as disguise she takes,
His knees tremble, His head turns grey and He dies.
Poor Dusk, under the mask she cries,
but mortals with eyes shut tight,
only see Dawn smiling bright.

And from the ashes of Master Wall, a new legend begins: that of The Pilgrim. A tale of scars and disharmony - another side of the same story.
