On the eve of War 2012-08-03 15:20 Fiction The Pilgrim

On the eve of the first battle of what later became known as the Dream Wars, people were scared, frightened: facing against uneven odds, and He has not been seen or heard of for days. Panic started to spread.

Seeing the enemy fires, their hearts became filled with a sense of imminent destruction. Their own demise. But there was no turning back now. That night, from within the shadows of the campfires He came - as always, unnoticed. He came as the dark between the flames, something that's always there, yet never is.

But when He spoke, they listened.

"I know you. I know you all too well: the fear in your hearts, the love you bear for one another, the courage you have, coming here against all odds. I heard you, and I came, but not to lead you into battle - you do not need me to lead. I came not to drive fear out of your hearts, for I cannot do that. I came to tell you what you've all seem forgotten, I came to scold you for the foolish ideas you got in your heads."

"I witnessed prayers, I heard people calling my name - as if I were a God! Let me assure you, I am not. You all are, and I am the tiny speck of dust in the great play. You make the world turn, You make my prayers come true! Who, if not the gods are the ones answering a man's prayer? You, my friends, are the Gods, not me. I am only a powerless man, with a wish. A wish, you are about to make come true. At Dawn, we will face the enemy that wants our dreams! Do Gods let their dreams fall apart? They do not!"

"Many will perish, but no matter how many of us they slaughter, our dreams never die. We will rise up, - from the grave, if we have to, - and fight, for neither dreams, nor Gods ever die!"

As the Legend says, this was the first time He dropped his hood, and people saw him as He truly was: a broken old man, grey hair, grey eyes, no more, no less.

"You came all the way, ready to go up against an enemy none could stand up against yet. You came, becuase you heard my prayer, and as great Gods as you are, you saw my dream, and saw that it was Your dream too. As long as the dream lives, there always will be a Dreamer."

People dreamt that night, and the battle was all but won.
