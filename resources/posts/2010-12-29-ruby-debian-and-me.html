Ruby, Debian and me 2010-12-29 16:05 Technology Rants

I'm a lazy person. When I'm at the computer, tinkering with something, I want to get done fast, I do not want to fight with the system, I just want to install whatever tools I need, and start hacking away.

For this reason, I strongly prefer installing packages from my distribution, globally, so that they are available to all of my projects. This is a fast and reliable way to get started, since my distribution of choice has fairly high quality packages. I trust the maintainers that they make sure that upgrades won't break existing software, and if a new upstream version comes out, which is not backwards compatible, they'll handle it in a sane way: create a new binary package. Security fixes can then be backported to the old version still in the distribution, and one is not forced to upgrade every component that uses a particular module, just because a security fix came out, and one had to upgrade to a completely new version.

Part of the reason I absolutely hate ruby nowadays is due to their stance of "We're a fast moving community. Keep up, or get out". It has often been my experience that many gems broke backwards compatibility without a warning, within a minor revision. I don't like that. I hate that. That forces me to update everything I wrote that uses said gem, because I'm sure as hell not going to keep a million different versions lying around on my system.

When I read stories such as this, which, I quote:

Under no circumstance should you install Ruby, Rubygems or any Ruby-related packages from apt-get. This system is out-dated and leads to major headaches. Avoid it for Ruby-related packages. We do Ruby, we know what's best. Trust us.

I get angry. They might do Ruby, but they're not distribution maintainers. It's not their area of expertise, and to be frank, they're doing a very poor job at that. I trust the Debian maintainers, because I know them, I have been one myself, and they made an amazing distribution, which haven't failed me since I first installed it in 1999. Ruby, and ruby gems failed me within an hour, and I should trust them? Bloody hell, NO.

If I wanted to compile everything myself, I would use a source based distribution. But I don't have time for that: I want my stuff to work, out of the box. That's why I use a distribution. And frankly, the Debian Ruby maintainers do hell of a good job! Working within such a hostile environment must be frustrating, yet, the quality of most ruby packages in Debian is fairly good.

I am using redmine (installed via apt-get), works fine, as long as I don't need the newer features. I tried to install a new version once, and after two hours of immense pain, I gave up. At that point, I made a vow: if I ever have to use anything ruby, it will be from the Debian repository. I'm never ever going to use RVM or gems or any of the other nonsense they came up with.

But better yet, I will try my best to steer clear of Ruby: however a good language it might be (though, I fail to see it's greatness), the community hates me, even though they don't know me. So, no thanks, no Ruby for me.
