Recruitment mistakes, part 2 2012-08-21 13:00 Rants

After the previous incident all seemed well. I did not get contacted again, so the plan worked. Sadly, I have to add a few items to the list posted earlier, as it seems the recruiting business is in desperate need of my sarcastic powers.

As a first rule of thumb, when you - the recruiter - mail a prospect, be sure to mention you found him through his stackoverflow profile, with about two questions answered, that have nearly no connection to the opportunities you will later mention. By no means mention the vast amount of other, relevant references available! The prospect may end up feeling good, and that leads to self-valuation, which means he'll ask for a higher salary. That would be bad, so don't do it!

Don't worry about the prospect having plans that would obviously conflict with working at your company. Money can buy anything, right?

And last, but not least, when the prospect replies to your mail within 24 hours, and is expecting a response, leave him hanging for at least a week. You're part of an enterprise business, things take time. He should be patient, and feel grateful even for having received your initial mail!

In case these guidelines do not have the desired effect, please consult the great guys over at RedHat, because they have first-hand experience in abiding these.
